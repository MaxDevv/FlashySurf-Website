The Core Loop: How It Works

As you browse the web, a flashcard will occasionally appear. Give it your best shot, learn from the instant feedback, and then get right back to what you were doing. Here's what you'll see in action:

Correct Answer ‚úÖ

You get a green check, a brief explanation, and the card closes in 5 seconds.

Incorrect Answer ‚ùå

You see the right answer, a detailed explanation, and have more time (20s) to review.

Heads-up: To avoid being annoying, there's always a 3-minute delay between questions. You'll never get bombarded.

Your Secret Weapon: The Notes Feature üìù

When you get a question wrong, you'll be prompted to take notes. This is the single most important feature for boosting your score.

Why This Is a Game-Changer:

  • It forces active learning. The magic happens when you rephrase the explanation in your own words. This builds deep understanding, not just memorization.
  • Don't BS this part! Putting in the effort to write 1-2 clear sentences about why you were wrong will pay off massively.
  • It builds a personalized study guide. You can review all your notes anytime in the extension popup.

Power User Tip üöÄ

Before a practice and the real test, copy your notes and feed them to an AI like ChatGPT with a prompt like: "Based on these notes from my study sessions, what are my biggest weaknesses and give me 5 last-minute tips."

üéØ Understanding the Collection Creator

The collection creator interface is powerful but straightforward. Here's how it works:

Key Components:

  • Collection Name: A descriptive name for your flashcard set (max 30 characters)
  • Question: The main question or prompt for each card
  • Paragraph: Optional context or reading passage for complex questions
  • Answer Choices: 2-4 multiple choice options (you can add/remove options dynamically)
  • Correct Answer: Select the right choice using radio buttons
  • Explanation: Optional but highly recommended - explains why the answer is correct

Collection Creation Best Practices üí°

  • Start small: Create 10-15 cards for your first collection to test it out
  • Write good explanations: These are crucial for learning, especially when you get answers wrong
  • Use varied question types: Mix definitions, applications, and conceptual questions
  • Test your collection: Create it, enable it, and see how it feels during browsing
